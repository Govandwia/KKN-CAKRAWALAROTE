Our Island

Rote — Indonesia's
Southernmost Gem

Rote Island sits at the very southern tip of Indonesia, closer to Australia than to Java. Home to the iconic Sasando harp, vast lontar palm groves, and some of the world's finest left-hand surf breaks, Rote is an island untouched by mass tourism — a place where authentic Indonesian island culture meets breathtaking natural beauty.

Culture & Traditions

🎵
Sasando
The iconic palm-leaf harp of Rote, one of Indonesia's most unique traditional instruments, crafted from lontar palm leaves.
🧶
Tenun Ikat Rote
Hand-woven ikat textiles with intricate geometric patterns, each piece telling a story of ancestry and island identity.
🎭
Ti'i Langga Dance
Traditional ceremonial dances performed during harvest festivals, wearing the iconic broad-brimmed Ti'i Langga hat.
🌴
Lontar Palm Culture
Every part of the lontar palm is used — from sap for traditional drinks to leaves for weaving, roofing, and instrument-making.
